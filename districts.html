Civic geography

Districts

Seven districts radiate from the Civic Heart, each with its own character and pace.

The districts are not separate. They fold into one another at their edges, so that the transition from one neighborhood to the next is felt as a gradual modulation rather than a border. Each contains everything a resident needs within walking distance.

District note

Paths between districts are porous. Transit, assemblies, and shared care mean that most residents regularly move beyond the place they sleep.

The shape of the city

Starting from the Civic Heart

District One houses the Learning Commons. District Three centers on nourishment and the river. District Five is the largest residential area. District Seven sits on the high terraces with views over the whole city.

Transit, assemblies, and shared care connect all seven districts. Most residents move across district lines daily. The map below shows proximity, not division.

The Civic Heart

The Civic Heart

Central halls, the great commons, evening music, archival readings, and the main inter-district transit hub. Most residents pass through daily.

Performances, presentations, and community gatherings are concentrated here in the evenings. The Assembly Hall and Open Air Stage both have open entry.
